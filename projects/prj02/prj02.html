Citizen science and land cover classification

Summary

The earthâ€™s surface is often complex and heterogeneous. Forcing this complexity into relatively low-level categorical classes is prone to errors and disagreements. When you allow for subjective interpretation of terms, the consistency and reliability of responses drop and the variety of unique responses increases. Overall, generally, humans are successful in agreeing upon Developed, Forested, and Water land cover classes, and are poor in agreeing upon classes like Shrubs and Grassland. When using citizen science, making the task as objective as possible is critical for reliable and consistent responses.

Human participants were asked to make a series of land cover classifications given on-the-ground images and aerial images of the earth's surface. Participants were presented these images and asked to choose 1 of 11 land cover classes for each image.
